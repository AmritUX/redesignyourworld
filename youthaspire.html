Designing a resourceful website for youths seeking careers in Ontario


An impactful non-for-profit project of First Work to help Canadian youth in career exploration. The goal was to develop relevant career related content for youth on Aspire’s website and twitter account.

Website:
youthaspire.org

Twitter:
@YthAspire








The Problem


  • There is a lack of career and related information online that is target towards youths in Ontario.
  • Many sources are giving misleading information about careers which causes biases and prejudices of certain jobs.
  • The internet is flooded with career information which causes youths in Ontario to be overwhelmed and picking the right career choice becomes a diffult task.




User Research


  • Youth lack self-esteem, self-confidence and need motivation for job hunting.
  • Youths in Ontario are provided with misleading career info and need a source that they can rely on for up-to-date and relevant info.
  • Youths in Ontario are misled by their parents to pursue careers they don’t want and end up with an unhappy job.
  • Youths in Ontario feel unaccepted in the workplace or employers won’t hire youth based on biases and prejudices.







The Solutions


  • Delivered to-the-point content related to careers and exploring career options for all Canadian youth.
  • Researched and developed specific content that is based on careers such as Digital Marketing and Advanced Manufacturing.
  • Added a recruitment section for employers so that they can host career exploration events for youths in Ontario.
  • Compiled all youth employment service providers info from First Work’s network with Google Map and locations
